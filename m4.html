Arabian Cuisine

Arabian cuisine is a soulful celebration of hospitality and spice.
Defined by fragrant grains, tender meats, and vibrant mezze, it balances
bold flavors like sumac and saffron with fresh herbs. Every dish reflects a rich history
of trade and the timeless tradition of sharing food with generosity.

Hummus

It is made by blending cooked chickpeas with tahini (toasted sesame paste), fresh lemon juice, and garlic. The result is a rich, nutty, and incredibly smooth spread.

View Ingredients
Cooked chickpeas, tahini, fresh lemon juice, garlic, extra virgin olive oil, sea salt, and cumin.
$9.50 Serves 1-2

Falafel

Traditionally made from soaked ground chickpeas (or fava beans) mixed with a vibrant bouquet of fresh parsley, cilantro, and garlic, they are spiced with cumin and coriander before being deep-fried to perfection.

View Ingredients
Dried chickpeas (soaked), fresh parsley, cilantro, onions, garlic, cumin, coriander, and sea salt.
$11.99 Serves 2

Tabbouleh

It is a vibrant, finely chopped salad where fresh flat-leaf parsley is the undisputed star, rather than the grain. Mixed with mint, juicy tomatoes, and crunchy scallions, it is lightly bound by a small amount of bulgur wheat.

View Ingredients
Finely chopped flat-leaf parsley, fresh mint, firm red tomatoes, scallions (spring onions), fine bulgur wheat, fresh lemon juice, extra virgin olive oil, and sea salt.
$10.99 Serves 2-3

Baba Ganoush

Baba Ganoush is a soulful, smoky dip made from eggplants that have been roasted over an open flame until the skin is charred and the flesh is buttery soft. This "smokiness" is the dish's signature, which is then blended with creamy tahini, garlic, and lemon juice.

View Ingredients
Fire-roasted eggplant, tahini, garlic, fresh lemon juice, sea salt, and extra virgin olive oil.
$8.99 Serves 1-2

Warak Enab

Warak Enab consists of tender grape leaves stuffed with a savory mixture of rice, finely chopped herbs, and aromatic spices. When prepared in the "bi-zeit" (with oil) style, they are strictly vegetarian, simmered slowly in a bath of lemon juice and high-quality olive oil until the leaves are silky and the rice is perfectly infused with citrus.

View Ingredients
Grape leaves (vine leaves), short-grain rice, fresh parsley, mint, tomatoes, onions, lemon juice, extra virgin olive oil, and a touch of Lebanese seven-spice.
$13.99 Serves 1-2

Shawarma

Shawarma is a culinary spectacle. It features thin cuts of marinated meat—typically lamb, beef, or chicken—stacked in a cone shape on a vertical rotisserie. As the meat slowly rotates against a heat source, the outer layer roasts to a perfect, spiced crisp while the interior remains incredibly succulent.

View Ingredients
Marinated meat (lamb, beef, or chicken), a spice blend (cumin, cardamom, turmeric, cinnamon, cloves), lemon juice, garlic, and vinegar. Served with tahini or garlic sauce.
$13.99 Serves 1-2

Manakish

Manakish (or Man’oushe) is a traditional flatbread where the dough is pressed with fingertips to create little indentations—perfect for holding flavorful toppings. The most classic version is topped with a generous amount of Za'atar (a savory blend of dried thyme, oregano, marjoram, toasted sesame seeds, and sumac) mixed with high-quality olive oil.

View Ingredients
Wheat flour dough, extra virgin olive oil, and toppings (Za'atar blend, Akkawi or Halloumi cheese, or spiced ground meat).
$13.99 Serves 2

Arayes

Arayes are essentially pita bread pockets stuffed with a thin, even layer of seasoned minced lamb or beef. Once stuffed, the pita is brushed generously with olive oil and then grilled or toasted until the bread becomes incredibly crispy and golden, while the meat inside steams in its own juices and spices.

View Ingredients
Fresh pita bread, minced lamb or beef, onions, parsley, and a blend of spices (typically allspice, cinnamon, and black pepper).
$13.99 Serves 1-2

Fatayer

Fatayer are small, handheld savory pies that are a staple of Levantine bakeries. They are most recognized by their distinct shapes: triangles for spinach (Sabanekh), boat-shapes for cheese (Jibneh), or open-faced rounds for meat.

View Ingredients
Soft wheat dough, fresh spinach or Akkawi/Feta cheese, onions, sumac, lemon juice, pine nuts, and extra virgin olive oil.
$16.00 Serves 2-3

Kibbeh

Kibbeh is a masterpiece of Mediterranean texture. It is a football-shaped croquette made from a dough of very finely ground lean beef or lamb mixed with soaked bulgur wheat, onions, and aromatic spices. This shell is stuffed with a "hashwi" (filling) of sautéed minced meat, onions, and toasted pine nuts.

View Ingredients
Fine bulgur wheat, lean ground beef or lamb, onions, pine nuts, and "Kibbeh spices" (allspice, cinnamon, cloves, and nutmeg).
$20.00 Serves 1-2

Kabsa

Kabsa is a majestic, fragrant rice dish that serves as the national dish of Saudi Arabia and is beloved across the Gulf. Unlike the constant stirring required for Risotto, Kabsa is prepared by simmering long-grain Basmati rice in a rich broth infused with a complex blend of "black" lime (loomi), cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, and bay leaves.

View Ingredients
Basmati rice, chicken or lamb, Kabsa spice mix (black lime, saffron, cardamom, etc.), onions, garlic, tomatoes, and toasted nuts.
$22.99 Serves 2

Mansaf

Mansaf is a dish of deep cultural significance, traditionally served on large circular platters to be shared by a group. It features tender chunks of lamb cooked in a rich, creamy sauce made from rehydrated Jameed. The meat and its flavorful sauce are served over a bed of yellow turmeric-stained rice, which sits atop a layer of thin Markook (shrak) flatbread.

View Ingredients
Lamb, Jameed (fermented dried yogurt), Basmati or short-grain rice, Markook bread, turmeric, and toasted nuts.
$22.99 Serves 2-3

Maqluba

Maqluba is a spectacular "one-pot" dish consisting of layers of meat (usually chicken or lamb), fried vegetables (typically eggplant, cauliflower, and potatoes), and aromatic rice. These are packed tightly into a pot and simmered until the flavors meld perfectly.

View Ingredients
Long-grain rice, fried eggplant, cauliflower, potatoes, chicken or lamb, and a spice blend of allspice, cinnamon, turmeric, and cloves.
$8.99 Serves 1-2

Shish Taouk

Shish Taouk is a masterclass in the art of the marinade. Cubes of chicken breast are soaked for hours in a mixture of yogurt, lemon juice, plenty of garlic, and a touch of tomato paste or paprika for color.

View Ingredients
Chicken breast, yogurt, garlic, lemon juice, olive oil, tomato paste, and a spice blend (paprika, oregano, cinnamon, and ginger).
$13.99 Serves 2-3

Kofta Kebab

Kofta Kebab consists of finely minced lamb or beef (or a blend of both) mixed with a generous amount of flat-leaf parsley, grated onions, and a signature blend of warm spices. The meat is molded by hand onto long, flat metal skewers, creating a distinct "rippled" shape, and then grilled over hot charcoals.

View Ingredients
Minced lamb or beef, fresh parsley, onions, garlic, and "Kofta spice" (typically allspice, cinnamon, black pepper, and sometimes a hint of chili).
$9.99 Serves 1-2

Shakshuka

Shakshuka is a rustic, one-pan dish that is as visually stunning as it is delicious. It features eggs poached directly in a thick, simmering sauce of stewed tomatoes, roasted bell peppers, onions, and garlic. The sauce is heavily spiced with cumin, paprika, and often a touch of chili for heat.

View Ingredients
Eggs, ripe tomatoes, red bell peppers, onions, garlic, tomato paste, cumin, smoked paprika, and extra virgin olive oil.
$12.99 Serves 1

Gahwa

Gahwa is much more than a drink; it is a symbol of generosity across the Arabian Peninsula. Unlike the dark, oily roast of Italian coffee, Gahwa uses lightly roasted (sometimes almost green) beans.

View Ingredients
Lightly roasted ground coffee beans, green cardamom pods, saffron threads, and sometimes cloves or rosewater.
$5.99 Serves 1

Mint Tea

Mint Tea is a cornerstone of social life across North Africa and the Levant. It is a specific blend of Chinese gunpowder green tea, a massive handful of fresh spearmint leaves, and a generous amount of sugar.

View Ingredients
Gunpowder green tea, large bunches of fresh spearmint, water, and sugar.
$5.99 Serves 1

Jallab

Jallab is a refreshing, dark amber beverage made from a syrup of dates, grape molasses, and rose water. It is diluted with water and served over a mountain of crushed ice. What makes Jallab truly unique is the "smoking" of the syrup with incense (mastic) and the garnish: it is traditionally topped with pine nuts and golden raisins.

View Ingredients
Date and grape molasses, rose water, water, crushed ice, pine nuts, and golden raisins.
$2.99 Serves 1

Ayran

Ayran is a simple yet genius blend of yogurt, water, and sea salt. It is whisked or churned until it develops a thin, frothy head and is served ice-cold. Unlike Western yogurt drinks that lean toward fruit and sugar, Ayran is crisp, tart, and salty.

View Ingredients
Fresh yogurt (cow or goat), chilled water, and a pinch of sea salt. (Sometimes garnished with dried mint).
$3.99 Serves 2

Baklava

Baklava is a triumph of geometry and patience. It consists of dozens of layers of paper-thin phyllo pastry, each brushed with clarified butter (ghee) and stacked with a generous filling of crushed pistachios, walnuts, or cashews.

View Ingredients
Phyllo dough, clarified butter, pistachios or walnuts, sugar, lemon juice, and orange blossom or rose water.
$9.99 Serves 1-2

Kunafa

Kunafa is a mesmerizing dessert made of a base of melted, stretchy Nabulsi or Akkawi cheese, topped with a crown of either crunchy, shredded phyllo pastry (Khishnah) or fine semolina crumbs (Na'ameh). It is baked in large circular trays until the top is golden and the cheese is molten.

View Ingredients
Shredded phyllo or semolina dough, Akkawi or Nabulsi cheese (often desalted), clarified butter (ghee), sugar syrup, rose water, and pistachios.
$10.99 Serves 2-3

Basbousa

Basbousa is a traditional Middle Eastern cake made from semolina, which gives it a distinct, slightly grainy, and satisfying crumb. The batter is often enriched with yogurt and desiccated coconut, then baked until the edges are caramelized.

View Ingredients
Semolina, yogurt, butter or ghee, sugar, coconut, and a lemon or orange blossom water syrup.
$4.99 Serves 1
