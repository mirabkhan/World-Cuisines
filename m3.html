Italian Cuisine

Italian cuisine is the art of simplicity, where high-quality ingredients speak for themselves.
Rooted in tradition and fresh flavors, it celebrates a perfect balance of sun-ripened tomatoes,
fragrant basil, and artisanal grains. From the hearth-baked pizzas of Naples
to the silky pastas of Rome, every dish is a tribute to the joy of gathering and
the timeless spirit of the Mediterranean.

Espresso

The heartbeat of Italian coffee culture, an Espresso is a concentrated, 1-ounce shot of pure coffee excellence. Defined by its intense aroma and a thick, hazelnut-colored crema on top, it is brewed by forcing pressurized hot water through finely-ground, dark-roast coffee beans.

View Ingredients
Highly concentrated coffee extracted from premium 100% Arabica or an Arabica-Robusta blend, and filtered water.
$5.99 Serves 1

Cappuccino

This classic beverage is built upon a foundation of a rich double shot of espresso, topped with equal parts steamed milk and a thick, velvety layer of frothed milk foam. A true Italian cappuccino is defined by its "micro-foam"—bubbles so small they create a silky, marshmallow-like consistency that naturally sweetens the coffee's bold profile.

View Ingredients
Concentrated espresso (100% Arabica or premium blend), fresh whole milk (steamed and frothed), and an optional dusting of cocoa powder or cinnamon on top.
$5.99 Serves 1

Gelato

Italy’s iconic gift to the world of frozen treats, Gelato is much more than just "ice cream." It is crafted with a higher proportion of milk to cream and churned at a much slower speed, resulting in a denser, silkier texture with significantly less air.

View Ingredients
Fresh whole milk, cream, sugar, and natural flavorings (such as slow-roasted nuts, fresh fruit purees, or premium cocoa).
$4.99 Serves 1

Cannoli Siciliani

This iconic dessert consists of a tube-shaped shell of fried pastry dough, flavored with a hint of cinnamon and cocoa, which is fried until golden and "blistered" for a characteristic crunch. The shell is filled to order with a sweet, creamy sheep’s milk ricotta filling.

View Ingredients

$7.99 Serves 2-3

Semifreddo

Unlike gelato, Semifreddo is not churned; instead, it is made by folding together a rich custard or zabaglione with whipped cream. This process traps air, creating a frozen treat that is light, airy, and velvety.

View Ingredients
Whipped heavy cream, eggs (sugar-whipped yolks or meringue), and flavorings such as honey, toasted hazelnuts, dark chocolate, or seasonal fruit purees.
$11.99 Serves 1-2
